## Purpose

Hs-24 tests whether the pipeline produces physically meaningful diagnostics when applied to published, peer-reviewed high-energy physics measurements that were never designed for compositional analysis.

## Summary

Nine runs across eight curated systems. Four classify NATURAL (Higgs branching ratios, top quark helicity, cosmic energy budget, CKM matrix elements). Four classify FLAG (W decay, tau decay, Z decay, proton momentum fractions). One run failed at Guard 4 (CKM first attempt — extreme hierarchy), was fixed, and succeeded on rerun.

## Key Findings

1. **FLAG separates counting from dynamics.** The four FLAG systems are governed by degree-of-freedom counting (colour channels, weak isospin states). The four NATURAL systems involve genuine compositional dynamics (mass-dependent couplings, gravitational evolution, flavour mixing). The pipeline discovers this distinction from geometry alone.

2. **SM-CPL-DIS is universal in HEP.** Carrier coupling fired in all 8 successful runs. Conservation laws (probability conservation, unitarity, angular momentum) force carrier coupling. The pipeline detects it without knowing it exists.

3. **Transcendental constants map to physics.** Four matches from four different families: ζ(2) for Higgs (sum-of-squares coupling), Khinchin for top (hierarchical partition), 1/(e^π) for cosmic (exponential partition), Lambert ω for CKM (fixed-point structure).

4. **EITT failure is diagnostic for hierarchy.** The sole EITT failure (CKM) has the steepest carrier hierarchy (5 orders of magnitude). Entropy decimation loses signal from small carriers. EITT failure diagnoses hierarchy depth.

5. **Bowl shape is universal for published data.** 8/8 bowls. Variance accelerating. Well-sampled, well-measured datasets always show increasing compositional information.

## What This Experiment Proves

The pipeline reads real physics. Classifications correlate with the physics of each system. Structural modes reflect genuine compositional properties. The catalog system produces self-indexing databases of analyses. Guard 4 protects against extreme hierarchy.

## Implications for Advancement

1. **Scale to full HEPData archive.** Thousands of published branching ratios and decay widths are available. Systematic scanning would test FLAG/NATURAL separation across all Standard Model processes.

2. **Test transcendental constant families across decomposition strategies.** Does the same system match different constants under different carrier definitions? This tests whether the match is robust or decomposition-dependent.

3. **Build a HEP fingerprint atlas.** Compositional fingerprints for all Standard Model processes would enable cross-process geometric comparison — finding structural homology between different particle decays.

4. **Apply to BSM (Beyond Standard Model) predictions.** If a proposed new particle's branching ratios differ from Standard Model predictions, does the fingerprint shift? The pipeline could detect anomalies as geometric deviations from the SM fingerprint baseline.

5. **The FLAG/NATURAL boundary as physics classifier.** Formalise the observation that FLAG = combinatorics and NATURAL = dynamics. Test against known cases in condensed matter, chemistry, and biology.
