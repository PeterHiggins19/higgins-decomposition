The Standard Helix — Calibration Reference

Hs-M01 Manifold Calibration | Compound Test Signal

The helix is the fundamental compound signal: drift + oscillation. It combines the linear drift of the M01 Line (directional movement across the simplex) with the periodic oscillation of the M01 Circle (rotation in CLR space). Most real-world compositional transitions look like helices — a secular trend with cyclical variation around it.

Helix = Line + Circle CLR construction (D=3, N=80): c1(t) = drift * t + radius * cos(omega * t) c2(t) = radius * sin(omega * t) c3(t) = -(c1 + c2) // zero-sum constraint x = closure(exp(c)) // map to simplex

4-Part Helix (S³ Simplex)

The helix generalises to any dimension. The S³ helix (D=4) adds a phase-shifted third oscillation axis, producing a richer compound signal in 4-part compositional space.

D=4, N=80, drift=0.015, radius=0.25, 4 rotations c1(t) = drift*t + radius*cos(omega*t) c2(t) = radius*sin(omega*t) c3(t) = radius*cos(omega*t + PI/3) c4(t) = -(c1+c2+c3)
