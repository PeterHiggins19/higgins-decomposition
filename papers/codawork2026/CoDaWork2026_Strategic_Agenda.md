## Q&A Updates — New Questions to Prepare For

The amalgamation work opens new Q&A territory:

**Q: Isn't amalgamation known to break subcompositional coherence?**
A: Yes — that is exactly the point. We test *what* breaks and *what* survives. The answer for the cosmic budget: classification is robust (100% preserved), but the conservation laws (black holes) become invisible inside amalgamated groups. The incoherence is the information — it tells you where the structure is hiding.

**Q: Why enumerate all possible amalgamations? Isn't that combinatorially explosive?**
A: For D=5 carriers, there are 25 non-trivial amalgamation schemes. For D=4, there are 10. These are small numbers. The enumeration is exhaustive and fast. For D>8 it becomes impractical, but most compositional datasets have D < 10.

**Q: Is the black hole / white hole terminology appropriate for CoDa?**
A: It is metaphorical but precise. A black hole is a zero-entropy information channel — the ratio never changes, no information is transmitted. A white hole is a maximum-entropy channel — the ratio is unconstrained. These map directly to the information-theoretic properties of the log-ratio pairs. The terms are vivid because the cosmic data is vivid. In other domains, "structural attractor" and "structural repeller" would serve.

**Q: What is the relationship between your amalgamation analysis and sequential binary partitions?**
A: Complementary. SBP gives orthogonal coordinates (balances) from a *chosen* partition. The T2 engine tests *all* partitions and asks which structural features are invariant across them. SBP is a coordinate system. T2 is a diagnostic that tests whether the answer depends on the coordinate system. If it does not — that is deep structure.

**Q: You said the operations are Aitchison's. What exactly is new?**
A: Five things: (1) Cumulative Aitchison variance as a *trajectory shape*, not a summary statistic. (2) The transcendental squeeze — testing proximity to mathematical constants. (3) EITT entropy invariance under geometric-mean decimation. (4) Subcompositional recursion as a *diagnostic tool* rather than a data reduction step. (5) The cross-domain fingerprint that enables compositional comparison across physics, economics, biology, engineering. The engine is CoDa. The vehicle is new.
