## Speaker Notes — Narrator Discipline

**Total spoken words:** ~310 (approximately 21 words per minute)

**The rule:** If the slide says it, Peter does not repeat it. Peter's voice adds context that is not on screen. The screen carries data, tables, visuals. Peter carries tone, pacing, and the three moments of silence.

**Three silences that matter:**
1. After "They live on your simplex." (Movement 2) — the audience claims ownership.
2. During the 7/7 cascade (Movement 2) — the evidence speaks.
3. After "Can anyone prove why?" (Movement 4) — the real ask.

**Physical direction:**
- Stand to the side of the screen, not in front of it.
- Face the audience when speaking, face the screen when the demo runs.
- Hands still. No laser pointer. The audience can read.
- For the live demos: open laptop, hit play, step back. Let the system run.

**What NOT to do:**
- Do not explain what is on the slide. They can read.
- Do not apologise for being an engineer. The work speaks.
- Do not rush to fill silence. Silence is the most powerful tool in the room.
- Do not touch the laptop during the duality dance. Let it run its full 2 minutes.
- Do not say "as you can see" — everything on screen is visible. That phrase is noise.

**The parentage:** Aitchison built the simplex. Pawlowsky-Glahn and Egozcue built the coordinates. Martín-Fernández built the zero handling. The CoDaWork community built the culture. Hˢ is the child of this lineage. Peter is the parent who brings the child to meet the grandparents. He does not need to explain the family resemblance. They will see it.
