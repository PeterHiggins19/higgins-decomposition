## Q&A Preparation (5 minutes)

### Anticipated Questions and Prepared Responses

**Q: Isn't the transcendental match just the Intermediate Value Theorem?**
A: Yes — existence is guaranteed by IVT. That is why the match alone is necessary, not sufficient. The discriminator is match DENSITY. Natural systems show 39× higher match density than uniform random (326 vs 8.4 per unit trajectory) and 5.6× concentration (0.45 vs 0.08 Herfindahl). We agree IVT is real. We measure the pattern, not the existence.

**Q: Why CLR and not ILR?**
A: CLR preserves the connection between each component and the compositional centroid. ILR compresses to a basis. For an instrument that reads per-carrier contributions, CLR retains interpretability. But your question is exactly the test we want: run ILR and see if the transcendental resonance survives. If it does, it is in the data. That experiment has not been done.

**Q: The 1/(e^π) match for the cosmic budget — is that physically meaningful?**
A: The pipeline found it. The proximity is δ = 4.19 × 10⁻⁵. Whether it reflects deep geometric structure in Friedmann cosmology or is a coincidence of the log-ratio architecture is an open question. We report it as a detection (point of interest), not as established physics. The honest answer is: we do not know yet.

**Q: How do you handle zeros?**
A: Multiplicative replacement at 10⁻⁶, standard CoDa practice (Martín-Fernández et al., 2003). The input guard rejects data with extreme scale disparity (max/min > 10¹⁵) to prevent log-ratio distortion. For the cosmic budget, neutrino fractions at 10⁻⁵ are well within bounds.

**Q: What is new here vs. standard CoDa analysis?**
A: The pipeline itself uses standard CoDa transforms — that is the point. What is new: (1) the cumulative Aitchison variance trajectory as a diagnostic shape, (2) the transcendental squeeze against 35 constants, (3) the EITT decimation test, (4) the Component Power Mapper that identifies disproportionate carriers, (5) the fingerprint system that enables cross-domain comparison, (6) the deterministic audit trail. The engine is CoDa. The vehicle is new.

**Q: Can we try it on our own data?**
A: Yes. `python hs_ingest.py yourdata.csv --all-languages`. Any CSV of compositions. Column headers become carriers. The tool handles closure, zero replacement, and everything else. The repo is public.

**Q: What about the EITT proof — have you tried to prove it?**
A: We have an empirical result (< 5% entropy variation across 25 experiments) and an intuition (the geometric mean is the natural centre of the Aitchison simplex, so geometric-mean decimation is a natural subsampling). But the formal proof connecting Shannon entropy preservation to simplex geometry does not exist. That is why we are here. This community knows the simplex better than anyone.
